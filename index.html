STARTING IN PLAIN ENGLISH

What is OSINT?

A craft — not a tool. Public information, collected with discipline, reasoned into evidence.

OSINT is the craft of answering investigative questions using information that anyone can legally access — the open web, social media, public records, news, leaked databases — then turning that raw information into evidence a decision-maker can act on.

It is not hacking.

No unauthorised access. Only information that is lawfully available to anyone.

It is not guessing.

Every claim must be sourced, dated, and reproducible by another analyst.

It is not free intel.

It is disciplined collection, verification, and analysis. The discipline IS the value.

Module 1 · Introduction to OSINT6 / 67

WHERE THE TERM COMES FROM

A working definition.

The 2006 US National Defense Authorization Act gave OSINT its formal definition. NATO adopted the same framing.

"Information that is publicly available, collected, exploited, and disseminated in a timely manner to an appropriate audience for the purpose of addressing a specific intelligence requirement."

— US NDAA 2006 · also NATO AJP-2.0

Not new.

Intelligence services have monitored newspapers, radio & journals for over a century. FBIS was founded in 1941.

Transformed by the internet.

Billions now publish daily. The signal and the noise have both exploded — by orders of magnitude.

Operationally decisive.

Public estimates put OSINT at 70–90% of the input to finished intelligence products today.

SURFACE · DEEP · DARK

Open source ≠ open access.

Most useful OSINT for a corruption case lives in the deep web — public, but unindexed.
SURFACE WEB~4%
Indexed by Google & Bing. News, blogs, most public social posts. The visible tip.
DEEP WEB~90%
Public but not indexed — academic databases, court filings, corporate registries, gated records, paywalled archives.
DARK WEB~6%
Tor / I2P only — illicit markets, leak forums, ransomware portals, sanctioned-actor chatter.

A REPEATABLE DISCIPLINE

The OSINT cycle.

Five stages. Iterative — every analysis raises new questions that feed the next plan.
01
PLAN
Define the question (PIR). Who, what, where, by when.
02
COLLECT
Gather from sources mapped to the question. Preserve originals.
03
PROCESS
De-duplicate, translate, extract, structure into entities & events.
04
ANALYZE
Corroborate, connect, interpret. Write with evidence.
05
DISSEMINATE
Deliver to the decision-maker in usable form. Close the loop.
The PIR template: Subject · Question · Decision · Indicators · Boundary · Deadline. Six fields up front prevents 80% of wasted-effort failures.

HOW NATO & INTEL COMMUNITIES GRADE EVIDENCE

The Admiralty Code.

A 6×6 grid: source reliability × information credibility. Record both for every artefact.
Source reliability
ACompletely reliable
BUsually reliable
CFairly reliable
DNot usually reliable
EUnreliable
FCannot be judged
Information credibility
1Confirmed by other sources
2Probably true
3Possibly true
4Doubtful
5Improbable
6Cannot be judged
Example: A Twitter post from a verified journalist citing a court document → B-2. An anonymous forum claim with no corroboration → F-6. Court submission usually requires at least two independent A2 / B2 findings.

PAKISTAN CONTEXT

Legal & ethical boundaries.

NAO 1999
Empowers NAB to investigate wilful default and corruption. §27 enables information collection from any source — squarely covers OSINT.
AMLA 2010 (as amended)
Anti-Money Laundering Act. §7 enables FMU access to public & reporting-entity data. Covers predicate offences and STR review.
PECA 2016
Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act. §3 (unauthorised access), §29–32 (data privacy). OSINT must stay on the public-and-lawfully-accessed side.
Investigation Act 2022
Federal investigation framework. Chain-of-custody and digital-evidence handling baseline.
Qanun-e-Shahadat 1984
Evidence law. Article 164 permits electronic evidence with integrity safeguards (hashing, chain of custody).
Golden rule: if you have to bypass a technical or legal control to get it — it is not OSINT.

CHAIN OF CUSTODY · HASHING · ADMISSIBILITY

Making OSINT court-ready.

Capture properly

Use Hunchly or a forensic browser capture — not naked screenshots. Preserve URL, timestamp, full-page render.

Hash everything

SHA-256 every artefact at collection. Log the hash in your case file before any further processing.

Maintain the artefact log

Timestamp · source URL · file · hash · Admiralty rating · pivot it produced. Same row format every time.

Reference Article 164

Qanun-e-Shahadat 1984 Art. 164 makes electronic evidence admissible — provided integrity is demonstrable.

Defensibility test: can a competent third party reproduce your finding from your log? If yes, it will hold up in NAB court.

WHERE IT WILL HURT YOU IF YOU ARE NOT CAREFUL

Limits & pitfalls.

Confirmation bias

Looking only for evidence that supports the theory.

Stale data

Information that was true a year ago may be stale today.

Impersonation

Parody / fake / impersonator accounts taken as the subject.

AI fabrication

Don't trust LLM summaries without checking each source.

Over-reach

Crossing into PECA-restricted territory destroys the case.

Single source

One social-media post is suggestive — never conclusive.

Operating principle: OSINT is corroboration, not conclusion. Every finding must be second-sourced before it goes into a court submission.

YOUR OLDEST OSINT TOOL

Google dorking.

Six operators — site, filetype, intitle, inurl, exact-match, OR — get to the artefact in five iterations.
# Site-restricted search
site:acme-trading.pk

# Find disclosed PDFs / spreadsheets
site:acme-trading.pk filetype:pdf
site:acme-trading.pk (filetype:xls OR filetype:xlsx OR filetype:csv)

# Indexed directories
intitle:"index of" site:acme-trading.pk

# Subject across regulator / news sites
"Mr. Subject Name" (site:secp.gov.pk OR site:nab.gov.pk OR site:dawn.com)

# Email exposure on paste sites
"target@acme-trading.pk" site:pastebin.com
Don't stop at Google. Run the same dorks on Bing, DuckDuckGo, and Yandex. Yandex indexes Russian and Central Asian sites better — useful for cross-border ML.

INFRASTRUCTURE TELLS STORIES PEOPLE DON'T

Domain · WHOIS · DNS · CT.

WHOIS

Registrant name (or privacy proxy), org, registrar, registration date, name servers. Privacy services hide names but rarely registration date — a strong operational signal.

DNS

A, MX, TXT records reveal hosting provider, mail provider, infra patterns. Same MX across multiple "unrelated" companies is a tell.

Certificate Transparency

crt.sh / Censys log every public TLS cert issued. Includes "wildcard SANs" — sometimes 50+ domains under a single operator.

Wayback Machine

CALL DETAIL RECORDS — THE WHO / WHEN / HOW-LONG

What is a CDR?

What it captures

Per call: A-party, B-party, start time, duration, IMEI, IMSI, cell tower (LAC + CID), call type. Generated by every operator in real time.

What it is not

It is metadata, not content. CDRs do not contain call audio — that requires lawful intercept, not OSINT.

How investigators get it

Through formal request to the operator under NAO §27 / AMLA §7. Typically delivered as CSV / Excel — millions of rows.

Legal framing: CDR access in Pakistan is statutorily controlled. Never analyse a CDR you have not received through proper case authority.

COMMON MISTAKES THAT LOSE CASES

CDR / IPDR pitfalls.

Tower ≠ location

Cell coverage overlaps. Same tower implies proximity, not co-presence in the same room.

SIM ≠ identity

Who held the SIM at call time? CNIC-binding helps but isn't proof of physical custody.

Time-zone slip

Operator exports in UTC; analyst overlays in PKT. One bad join = wrong narrative.

Schema drift

Each operator's column names differ. Re-validate the schema on every new export.

Sample bias

Asking only for one subject misses peer SIMs. Request the network around the subject.

Admissibility gap

Hash the export, log the chain. Without it, defence motion can exclude the entire dataset.

PERSONAS ARE A TOOL · NOT A COSTUME

Sockpuppets & investigative personas.

Build over time

Real personas are aged at least 60 days before active use. Sudden creation + immediate target contact is a red flag to any platform.

Consistent attribution surface

Same email pattern, same VPN endpoint, same browser fingerprint. Drift in any of these breaks the persona.

One persona per case

Never reuse a persona across investigations — that links the cases for any future defence attorney.

Stay within entrapment lines

Observation only. Never solicit, never propose, never agree to a transaction. PECA + procedural law cover this strictly.
